    Automatic GCP Detection

    Ground Control Points (GCPs) are identifiable and accurately located points on the Earth's surface that serve as reference points to georeference or rectify imagery, maps, or other geographic data. It plays a crucial role in ensuring the accuracy and precision of geospatial data and is commonly used in applications like satellite imagery analysis, aerial photography, cartography, and geographic information systems (GIS). In aerial images, GCPs are employed with great precision to georeference images, meshes, and textures onto the surfaces of maps. Our main objective is to detect the GCPs automatically from the given input images and the geoinformation values.

    The Automatic GCP detection AI pipeline mainly uses Images as its data to carry the whole process. The raw data is the aerial image of very high resolution (approx 5000*3000 pixels). These images have lots of other features like buildings, cranes, towers, cars, trees, roads potholes which are not our primary concerns. The main focus is to detect the GCP within the image.

    The different steps taken are:
    • Data Preprocessing: The whole image is of very high resolution. If the whole images are fed into the AI pipeline to detect GCP (which is very small as compared to the whole image) (approx 1-3 pixels) in the above pictures then we will have the following problems:
      1. Maximum Resources used while model training (both time and memory): More CUDA-enabled resources are needed which cannot be handled by the normal GPU of 4 to 8 GB.
      2. Highly inaccurate prediction due to smaller bounding box regions in the images: During the Feasibility Test on using it with different models like YOLO, ResNet, and FastRNN gave wrong predictions.
    • Following actions were taken for preprocessing:
      1. Formation of cropped images using the geoinformation
      2. Data Annotation
      3. Data Augmentation
    • Model Training and evaluation
    • Product development: This function is all fully dockerized and when user uploads GCP notify the process throgh SLACK and upload the result to S3.
